## The Problem

Small businesses track customer relationships in a mix of notebooks, spreadsheets, and memory. When someone calls back six months later, the owner has no record of what was discussed, what was quoted, or whether they ever followed up.

## The Solution

A focused CRM with three core jobs: log every customer interaction, surface who needs a follow-up today, and keep a searchable history of every conversation. The backend is in a private repo; the public demo is a static frontend showcase with hardcoded seed data.

## Key Features

- **Pipeline Kanban** — drag and drop customers across five stages: Lead, Contacted, Quoted, Won, and Lost
- **Customer list** — searchable table filterable by name, company, or tag
- **Customer profiles** — contact info, pipeline stage, and full interaction history in one view
- **Interaction log** — record calls, meetings, emails, and notes with an optional follow-up date
- **Follow-ups dashboard** — groups all pending follow-ups into Overdue, Due Today, and Upcoming sections
- **Email reminders** — Resend-powered notifications when a follow-up comes due

## Technical Decisions

Data is persisted in PostgreSQL via Next.js API routes. Follow-up reminders are processed by a scheduled job that queries due reminders and sends emails via Resend.

Drag-and-drop on the Kanban is handled by `@dnd-kit` rather than the browser's native DnD API, which gives consistent behaviour across touch and pointer devices without fighting browser quirks.

The public demo repo runs entirely client-side with React Context and hardcoded seed data — no backend required to explore the UI.
